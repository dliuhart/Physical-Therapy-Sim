Simulation of a Shoulder II

There are four forces in the diagram, the joint reaction force JRF on the shoulder (the two components of JRF, JRFh and JRFv are labeled in red); the force that is exerted by the person’s deltoid muscle, Fdeltoid (orange), the weight of arm and hand WAH (blue) and the weight of ball Wball. (green). As the angle of the arm and the weight of the ball change, the arrows show the differences in the directions and magnitudes of forces.
Objectives:
  1. Find out how Fdeltoid, JRFh, JRFv and JRF depend on the mass of the ball.
  2. Observe how Fdeltoid, JRFh, JRFv and JRF change when the arm moves up and down; Discover the easiest and most challenging positions for the person.
  3. Be able to relate the simulations to life experience and explain the results by applying the knowledge of equilibrium.


Instructions:

According to your investigation case, you can replace initial values of the weight and height of the person. Similarly, the position of the arm and the weight of ball can be changed with the slider bars. Once the input is set up, you can click “Calculate” button to obtain the values of each force and its lever arm. Notes: The simulation is calculated based on a normally proportioned person. The weight of ball approximately acts on the hand, the end of the blue line.
